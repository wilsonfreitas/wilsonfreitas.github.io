Fórmula de Black&Scholes

$$ \begin{align} P_c(S,K,r,T,\sigma) & = S N(d_1) - K \mathrm{e}^{-rT} N(d_2) \\ P_p(S,K,r,T,\sigma) & = K \mathrm{e}^{-rT} N(-d_2) - S N(-d_1) \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} d_1 & = \frac{\log\left(\frac{S}{K}\right) + \left(r + \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)T}{\sigma \sqrt{T}} \\ d_2 & = d_1 - \sigma \sqrt{T} \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} \Delta_c & = N(d_1) \\ \Delta_p & = N(d_1) - 1 \equiv \Delta_c - 1 \end{align} $$

Fórmula de Black (1976)

$$ \begin{align} P_c(F,K,r,T,\sigma) & = \mathrm{e}^{-rT} \left( F N(d_1) - K N(d_2) \right) \\ P_p(F,K,r,T,\sigma) & = \mathrm{e}^{-rT} \left( K N(-d_2) - F N(-d_1) \right) \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} d_1 & = \frac{\log\left(\frac{F}{K}\right) + \frac{\sigma^2 T}{2}}{\sigma \sqrt{T}} \\ d_2 & = d_1 - \sigma \sqrt{T} \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} \Delta_c & = \mathrm{e}^{-rT} N(d_1) \\ \Delta_p & = \mathrm{e}^{-rT} ( N(d_1) - 1 ) \end{align} $$

Fórmula do Swaption

Esta fórmula é utilizada no apreçamento de contratos de opções sobre Futuros de DI1.

$$ \begin{align} P_c(F,K,r,T,\sigma) & = \delta \left( S^\prime N(d_1) - K^\prime N(d_2) \right) \\ P_p(F,K,r,T,\sigma) & = \delta \left( K^\prime N(-d_2) - S^\prime N(-d_1) \right) \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} d_1 & = \frac{\log\left(\frac{S^\prime}{K^\prime}\right) + \frac{\sigma^2 T_1^\prime}{2}}{\sigma \sqrt{T_1^\prime}} \\ d_2 & = d_1 - \sigma \sqrt{T_1^\prime} \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} \Delta_c & = N(d_1) \frac{PU_k}{PU_f} \\ \Delta_p & = ( N(d_1) - 1 ) \frac{PU_k}{PU_f} \\ PU_f & = 100000 \frac{PU_2}{PU_1} \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} K^\prime & = \frac{ (1 + k)^{T_2 - T_1} }{T_2^\prime - T_1^\prime} \\ S^\prime & = \left( \frac{PU_1}{PU_2} - 1 \right) \frac{1}{T_2^\prime - T_1^\prime} \\ \delta & = \frac{ PU_2 ( T_2^\prime - T_1^\prime ) } { 1 + K^\prime ( T_2^\prime - T_1^\prime ) } \end{align} $$

Fórmula de Garman-Kohlhagen

$$ \begin{align} P_c(S,K,r,T,\sigma) & = S \mathrm{e}^{-r_cT} N(d_1) - K \mathrm{e}^{-rT} N(d_2) \\ P_p(S,K,r,T,\sigma) & = K \mathrm{e}^{-rT} N(-d_2) - S \mathrm{e}^{-r_cT} N(-d_1) \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} d_1 & = \frac{\log\left(\frac{S}{K}\right) + \left(r -r_c + \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)T}{\sigma \sqrt{T}} \\ d_2 & = d_1 - \sigma \sqrt{T} \end{align} $$

$$ \begin{align} \Delta_c & = \mathrm{e}^{-rT} N(d_1) \\ \Delta_p & = \mathrm{e}^{-rT} ( N(d_1) - 1 ) \end{align} $$
